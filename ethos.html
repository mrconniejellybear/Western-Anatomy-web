Building an anatomy tool for the visual learner

In 2024, I began developing this site to offer a hands-on understanding of the brain to fellow students in neuroscience, anatomy, pre-med, biology, psychology, or related fields. I tried asking myself what tools I wish I had access to when I began studying the brain. In 2026, I debuted a demo at a psychology department conference, and the interest of students and professors motivated me to continue this resource.

It is the responsibility of every good scientist to not only explore and doccument, but to share that learning with the world. More eyes lead to more solutions. As such, it is my goal to keep this website free and open source, without paywall or log-in, so that it may reach as many people as possible in the pursuit of equal education. If you have any questions, please contact me via my links at the bottom of the page.

Although this program is my design, science is a collaborative process. People much smarter than me have explored and doccumented much of the brain, and any literature that informed my entries, as well as a complete index of the model, can be found on the references page.

Developed by Connor Mausolf - BS in progress, for Western Washington University Behavioral Neuroscience and General Psychology.
